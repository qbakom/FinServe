Three Business Problems & Proposed Solutions

Problem 1 — Manual Credit Memo Preparation

Credit & Operations staff (25 people) manually pull data from CRM and internal systems into Word/PDF templates to produce credit memos. Each memo takes 2–4 hours: gathering financials, computing ratios, writing the narrative, formatting, and routing for review. This creates bottlenecks at month-end, introduces copy-paste errors, and makes it hard to maintain consistent quality and risk language across analysts.

Solution — AI Credit Memo Generator

What: A web application that takes structured application data (client profile, financials, facility request), runs deterministic risk scoring (D/E, DSCR, LTV, leverage), and uses an LLM to generate a complete credit memo with executive summary, financial analysis, risk assessment, and recommendation — exportable as PDF.

How: FastAPI backend + Gemini API for narrative generation. Risk metrics are computed deterministically (no AI hallucination on numbers). AI only writes the narrative sections using verified data. Output matches the format the credit committee expects.

Impact: ~70% time reduction per memo → frees 5–10 FTEs worth of capacity. Consistent quality. Faster turnaround for clients.

Problem 2 — Unstructured Client Support

The 10-person support team answers tickets and emails individually without a shared knowledge base or standard responses. Common questions about loan status, repayment schedules, and document requirements get re-answered from scratch. Response times vary, tone is inconsistent, and new hires take months to ramp up. No analytics on ticket categories or resolution times.

Solution — AI Support Copilot with Knowledge Base

What: RAG-based system that indexes FinServe's product documentation, FAQs, policies, and past resolved tickets. When a new ticket arrives, it classifies it (status inquiry / complaint / document request / general), retrieves relevant context, and drafts a response for agent review. Auto-routes complex cases to the right specialist.

How: Embed documents into a vector store (e.g., Pinecone/pgvector). On each inbound ticket, classify intent, retrieve top-k relevant chunks, generate a draft reply. Agent reviews and sends — building a feedback loop that improves drafts over time.

Impact: 40–60% faster first response. Consistent messaging. Faster onboarding. Ticket analytics as a byproduct.

Problem 3 — Manual Regulatory Reporting

Finance & Risk teams (27 people combined) pull data from core banking and CRM into Excel for monthly, quarterly, and regulatory reports. This involves manual data extraction, reconciliation across systems, formula-heavy spreadsheets, and manual formatting to meet regulator templates. Errors are caught late, and the process consumes the last week of every quarter.

Solution — Automated Report Pipeline

What: An ETL pipeline that connects to core systems via API, reconciles data automatically, and generates reports in the required regulatory format. AI assists with anomaly detection (flagging unusual entries for human review) and narrative summaries for management commentary sections.

How: Python-based pipeline (Airflow/Prefect) pulling from core banking APIs → data validation & reconciliation → template rendering (Excel/PDF). LLM generates the management commentary. Anomaly detection via statistical rules + LLM explanation of flagged items.

Impact: Reporting cycle cut from 5 days to 1 day. Fewer errors. Audit trail built in. Staff freed for analysis over data wrangling.

Why I Built the Credit Memo Generator

CriterionRationale
Business impactTargets the largest department (25 people). Credit memos are the core deliverable of the lending business — every deal requires one. Even modest time savings multiply across hundreds of applications per year.
DemonstrabilityA credit memo is a tangible, reviewable artifact. The PoC produces a real document that a credit committee could evaluate — unlike a chatbot or pipeline that needs production data to demo.
Architecture shows judgmentThe design deliberately separates deterministic risk scoring (financial ratios, risk rating) from AI narrative generation. This is a critical design choice in financial services — you don't let an LLM compute your DSCR or decide your risk rating. AI writes the story; math computes the numbers.
ExtensibilityThe same architecture extends naturally to proposal generation (Sales), KYC summary reports (Compliance), and portfolio reviews (Risk).

Design Decisions

  • Deterministic risk scoring: Financial ratios and risk ratings are computed by code, not by AI. This is non-negotiable in financial services — you need reproducible, auditable risk numbers.
  • AI for narrative only: The LLM receives pre-computed metrics and writes the human-readable analysis. It cannot change the numbers, only interpret them.
  • Structured output: The LLM is prompted to return JSON with defined keys, ensuring consistent memo structure across all applications.
  • Sample data: Three realistic scenarios included (strong SME, moderate agricultural firm, high-risk retail startup) to demonstrate range of outputs.
